What are Mimes

The Mimes Are historically Roman entertainers, now associated with France, that seek to please the masses with often humorous performances invloving a lack of sound. Mimes traditionally take a vow of silence at the age of 22, or whenever they decide to commit to the lifestyle. They can only talk while in a mime exclusive area, or when alone.

What do they do?

The average mime roams the streets of their European hometown, performing their art on the streets. They make a living off of donations given to them by generous passerbiers, who throw any loose change in their pockets on the cobbles in front of the mimes. The best of the mimes instead perform on stages, often in front of wealthy patrons or higher ranking officials. To keep with tradition, the money the make is still thrown onto stage.

How to identify a mime

To identify a mime, you can look for a few things. While it may be difficult to tell them apart from mute schizophrenics or someone having a bad trip, there are some key ways to identify them.

Some traits of mimes are:

  • Intricate facepaint and body paint, creating a unique pattern for each mime
  • Primarily black and white colors, with only a few highlights of color.
  • Their performance leaves you feeling a sense of dread or wonder
  • Percise physical movements
  • A deep smell of fresh bread lingers in the air
  • You feel an odd sense of attraction

Traits of non mimes:

  • They are holding a bottle of beer. Mimes only drink on sundays
  • They have messy movement
  • They speak
  • They aren't wearing clothes
  • They smell like feces
